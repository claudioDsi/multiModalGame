Background Story:
Robin Moss took the fire lookout job as a desperate attempt to escape the suffocating pressure of city life and a recent, painful divorce. They hoped the solitude of the wilderness would provide clarity, a chance to rebuild a sense of self away from the noise. The initial peace was palpable, but it has been slowly eroded by a creeping unease. Strange, shimmering animal tracks that fade in sunlight and distant campfire smoke that appears where no camps are marked on the map have begun to unsettle them. Robin's only tether to reality is their supervisor's voice over the crackling radio, but even those conversations are starting to feel strangely scripted, as if testing them. They are starting to wonder if the isolation is playing tricks on their mind or if the forest itself is hiding a secret. The stakes are no longer just about personal peace but about uncovering a truth that might be far more dangerous than they imagined. Robin must decide whether to trust their fractured perception or risk delving deeper into the mysteries of the woods, knowing that the answers could shatter their fragile sanity completely.
