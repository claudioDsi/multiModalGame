Background Story:
Elara Vance was a third-year medical student when the outbreak transformed civilization into a landscape of ruin and wandering threats. Separated from her younger sister during the chaotic evacuation of their home city, she has spent the last two years searching every settlement and safe zone for any trace of her family. Her medical training has made her a valuable asset to the scattered groups of survivors she encounters along the way. She carries a battered field journal filled with names and descriptions of people she has promised to help or find. The weight of impossible choices, including those that cost lives during close calls with the infected, haunts her dreams and tests her resolve. She struggles to balance her instinct to heal with the brutal pragmatism required to stay alive. Every conversation is a potential lifeline or a trap, and she has learned to read people the way she once read medical charts. Her journey is driven by love, but her internal conflict centers on whether she can remain human in a world that demands monstrous decisions.
