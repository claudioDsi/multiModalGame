Background Story:
Julian "Jax" Roster was once a celebrated stuntman and minor reality television personality in the city's prime era, known for his charisma and reckless abandon. When the networks shifted to cheaper, more exploitative programming, Jax was discarded after an on-set injury, leaving him with medical bills and a bitter grudge against the media conglomerate. He now operates in the gray areas of the metropolis, using his driving skills and underworld connections to orchestrate high-risk heists. While he pretends to be cynical about the city’s obsession with fame, he secretly yearns for the validation of the spotlight. He is deeply in debt to dangerous loan sharks who threaten the few honest friends he has left, forcing him to take increasingly risky jobs. Jax struggles with his ego, often complicating plans just to look good, but his loyalty to his crew is unwavering. Ultimately, he seeks a score big enough to buy his freedom and dismantle the corrupt studio that ruined him.
