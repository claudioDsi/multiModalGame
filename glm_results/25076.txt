Background Story:
Silas Vane was once a loyal enforcer for the Hollowpeak Brotherhood, a brotherhood of outlaws that once ruled the untamed canyons of Brimstone Territory. Born to a saloon singer and a father he never knew, he learned early that survival meant loyalty to those with power. The Brotherhood raised him, taught him to shoot, and gave him purpose—until the railroads came. As the Iron Wardens, a powerful industrial consortium, laid tracks through the canyons, the Brotherhood's influence crumbled. Silas was ordered to burn a settlement that refused to sell its land, a line he refused to cross. He turned his back on the only family he ever knew, saving the town but earning a death mark from his former brothers. Now he rides east toward the coast, fleeing both the Brotherhood's assassins and the Wardens' hired guns. Every town he passes through is another step toward a freedom he's never truly known. Silas fights not for redemption—that word died with his innocence—but for a chance to disappear into a world that's leaving men like him behind.
