Background Story:
Silas Wren was a high school history teacher in a quiet rural town before the collapse, known for his storytelling and stubborn optimism. When the crisis erupted, he lost his wife and daughter while attempting to evacuate, a burden of guilt he carries in every decision he makes. He spent the first year wandering aimlessly until he encountered a group of survivors who reminded him that protecting others was the only way to honor his family's memory. Silas now serves as a reluctant leader and mediator, using his knowledge of history to find parallels between past conflicts and their current struggles. His internal conflict centers on whether he truly believes in hope or simply performs it for others' sake, and the weight of every life lost under his watch. The episodic journey forces him to confront moral compromises he never imagined making, from choosing who receives limited supplies to deciding whether to trust strangers bearing gifts. Each episode peels back another layer of his composed exterior, revealing a man desperate for redemption but unsure if he deserves it.
