Background Story:
Elias Thorne grew up in a quiet coastal town where days were measured by the rhythm of fishing boats and fog rolling in from the sea. After a series of personal losses and a relationship that ended in bitter silence, Elias made the impulsive decision to spend a summer as a fire lookout in the remote mountain wilderness, seeking solitude and a chance to think. The job seemed simple enough: watch for smoke, report anything unusual, and maintain the tower. But as the weeks stretched on, Elias began noticing odd patterns in the forest—unexplained lights at night, strange radio interference, and footprints that appeared where no one should have walked. The only lifeline to the outside world is a voice on the other end of a handheld radio, a supervisor stationed at another tower, whose casual observations gradually turn into concerned questions about what Elias might be seeing. Elias must navigate not only the physical dangers of the wilderness but also the creeping sense that something larger is at play, something that may be connected to the very reasons for fleeing to the mountains in the first place. The summer becomes a journey of confronting old wounds while questioning what is real and what is merely the echo of a troubled mind seeking patterns in chaos.
