Background Story:
Dravos Ironhand was once the champion of the Grand Colosseum, undefeated for seven seasons until a corrupt noble paid his own stablemaster to ensure a losing match. Left for dead in the aftermath and sold into indentured servitude, Dravos spent years in the fighting pits of the outer provinces, honing his craft and biding his time. He eventually earned his freedom through a series of brutal exhibition matches, emerging with a reputation as a fighter who never surrenders and never breaks. Now returned to the capital city, Dravos seeks to build his own gladiator school from nothing, recruiting society's castoffs and forging them into champions. He is driven by a desire for vengeance against the noble who wronged him, but struggles with the morality of sending others into the arena to achieve his goals. The memory of every fighter he has lost haunts him, and he carries a small stone token from his first fallen recruit as a reminder of the cost of ambition. Dravos believes that glory earned through honor is the only glory worth having, even in a world that rewards treachery.
